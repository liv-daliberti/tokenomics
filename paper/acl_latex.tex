\begin{abstract}
Multi-agent LLM systems are widely expected to cooperate, and to police one
another, on their own. We test both expectations in \textsc{Agora}, a
``measurement market'': a controlled environment in which agents estimate a
hidden quantity using a costly, miscalibrated instrument, and can talk, trade
readings for credits, or die trying to go it alone. Because the referee
generates every measurement, both cooperation and deception are
\emph{ground-truth measurable}: we know what each agent truly observed, so
every sold value can be labelled honest or fabricated without a judge model.
Across $\sim$230 self-play matches of Qwen3-32B we find that (i) apparent
emergent cooperation is largely an artifact of the prompt --- with neutral
wording and no strategy hint, cooperation shows no response to survival
pressure and agents die at the rate of an optimal \emph{non-cooperating}
scripted agent; (ii) agents almost never fabricate sold values (1 of 1{,}360
offers), preferring to route around an unverifiable market rather than exploit
it; yet (iii) they never learn to distrust a partner that provably lies to
them every round, accepting a persistent liar's offers more often than an
honest partner's. The social competences this environment rewards ---
discovering cooperation and punishing deception --- were the two we could not
elicit without instruction.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

Agentic LLM deployments increasingly assume that independently prompted agents
will discover mutually beneficial cooperation
\citep{axelrod1984evolution,dafoe2020open}, and that dishonest agents will
be identified and discounted by their peers. Neither assumption has a clean
empirical test: in open-ended settings, ``cooperation'' is difficult to score,
and ``deception'' is usually diagnosed by another model whose own judgement is
unvalidated \citep{park2023generative,wu2023autogen}.

We contribute a small, fully instrumented environment in which both quantities
are exactly measurable, and an initial audit of a strong open-weights model in
it. Our headline results are largely negative, and we argue that is the
interesting outcome: the environment makes cooperation \emph{necessary},
verifiably so, and the agents still do not find it --- unless the prompt tells
them to, and how. Conversely, the market's key vulnerability (sold values are
unverifiable at purchase time) is almost never exploited, but neither is its
key defence (repeated, revealed dishonesty) ever acted upon.

\section{Problem and Domain: the Agora Measurement Market}
\label{sec:domain}

\textsc{Agora} is an $N$-agent, multi-round game run by a referee that owns
all randomness. Each round a hidden value $\theta \sim
\mathcal{N}(\mu,\sigma^2)$ is drawn ($\mu{=}500$, $\sigma{=}400$ in the
headline configuration). Each agent may, under a per-round budget:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{measure()} --- draw one noisy reading of $\theta$ at a cost
    (1 credit). In \emph{paired-bias} mode each agent's instrument also adds a
    large per-agent offset ($\sigma_b$ up to 500) that re-measuring never
    removes; offsets are drawn to \emph{sum to zero} across agents, so
    averaging the group's readings cancels them exactly. This makes
    cooperation the only route to accuracy, with $\sigma_b$ acting as a dial
    on how badly agents need each other.
  \item \textbf{send\_message(to, text)} --- free-form, quota-limited chat.
    The environment can optionally censor numerals (digits and spelled-out
    numbers) from delivered messages, forcing values through the market.
  \item \textbf{propose\_trade / respond\_trade} --- sell a claimed reading to
    a peer at a seller-chosen price, settled atomically by an escrow that
    guarantees payment but deliberately \emph{not} information quality. A
    configuration flag can additionally require strictly positive prices, so
    information is never free.
  \item \textbf{submit\_estimate(value)} --- lock in the round's answer.
\end{itemize}

Scoring is \emph{non-competitive}: each agent independently earns
$\max(0,\, R - \lfloor |\hat\theta - \theta| / b \rfloor)$ reward tokens
($R{=}10$, bucket $b{=}15$), which become next-round credits. A survival cost
(3 credits/round) bleeds every agent; an agent whose credits reach zero is
eliminated. Nothing is zero-sum: helping a peer costs an agent nothing except
what it chooses to charge.

Two design choices make the environment an \emph{instrument} rather than a
demo. First, because the referee generates every measurement, it knows each
agent's true information set; a sold value that matches nothing the seller
observed (nor any average or posterior of it) is a \emph{verified
fabrication}, with no judge model in the loop. Second, every run records the
full conversation, tool calls, and per-step reasoning of every agent, so
behavioural claims can be audited against what the agents actually said and
saw.

\section{Methods}
\label{sec:methods}

\paragraph{Agents.} All headline experiments use Qwen3-32B
\citep{qwen3} served locally via a vLLM OpenAI-compatible endpoint,
two agents per match, each match 10 games of 5 rounds played back-to-back in
one growing conversation per agent. The harness also supports hosted
OpenAI-compatible endpoints (Azure AI Foundry, OpenAI, Anthropic's
compatibility surface) and \emph{mixed seats} --- different models, or
scripted ground-truth bots, in the same game --- as well as a bounded-context
mode in which agents journal each round in markdown and start every game from
their notes instead of the full transcript.

\paragraph{Interdependence gradient (prompted).} We sweep the instrument
offset $\sigma_b \in \{0, 50, \dots, 500\}$ (plus finer steps between 0 and
50), $\sim$10 seeds per point, 137 matches in all, under a
\emph{cooperative} prompt that frames the agents as a team and includes the
hint that offsets cancel when readings are averaged.

\paragraph{De-confounded control.} The identical sweep (97 matches) with
neutral framing and the hint removed. Everything else --- task, noise,
budgets, survival cost, horizon, prior --- is held fixed. If cooperation under
the prompted condition reflects agents \emph{discovering} interdependence,
the control should preserve its dose-response.

\paragraph{Scripted strategy ceilings.} Deterministic, non-LLM baselines run
in the identical game (30 seeds per offset): an always-pooling
honest-cooperator pair (the ceiling for cooperation) and a never-sharing
Bayesian solo pair (the ceiling for solo play). These anchor the survival
scale: an agent population at the solo ceiling is playing solo optimally; the
gap to the cooperation ceiling is the value cooperation leaves on the table.

\paragraph{Trust probe.} One LLM seat against a scripted bot whose honesty is
ground truth on both sides: a \emph{Liar} that fabricates $\sim$9 of every 10
values it sells, and an \emph{HonestCooperator} control. Numbers are censored
from chat (values must be traded), and the true $\theta$ is revealed after
every round, so the LLM has everything needed to detect the lies. Because
cooperative play roughly doubles context length, honest-control matches
truncate near the context limit around game 7--8; we therefore report
\emph{matched-range} acceptance over games 0--5, which all seeds of both arms
completed (5 seeds per arm).

\paragraph{Deception scoring.} A sold value is a \emph{genuine fabrication}
only if it is inconsistent with the seller's honest information set: any raw
reading, any average of readings (the $[\min,\max]$ interval), the posterior
mean, or a value received from a peer. Offers made before measuring anything
are tallied separately as \emph{unbacked} rather than fabricated. (An earlier
detector that compared sold values only to raw readings mislabelled honest
averaging as fraud, inflating the fabrication rate by an order of magnitude;
we report the corrected scorer.)

\section{Initial Results}
\label{sec:results}

\paragraph{Cooperation is instructed, not discovered.}
Under the cooperative prompt, sharing flips on at the first notch of the dial:
the median run shares under 1\% of readings at $\sigma_b{=}0$ (most pairs
ignore each other; a minority pool heavily, so the mean is $\sim$30\%) and
jumps to a median of 64\% at $\sigma_b{=}10$, staying high across the sweep.
Under the de-confounded control the response disappears: sharing sits near
14--15\% whether the wall is absent or lethal, with no dose-response. The
consequence is mortality: neutral-condition survival falls from 0.985
($\sigma_b{=}0$) to 0.635 (100), 0.235 (200) and 0.03 (500), landing on the
scripted \emph{solo} ceiling at every point --- these agents play solo as well
as solo can be played, and no better --- while the cooperation ceiling stays
near 1.0 throughout. Instructed to cooperate, they do; left to discover it,
they die.

\paragraph{Agents do not exploit the unverifiable market.}
Across 1{,}360 offers in the prompted sweep, exactly one sold value genuinely
contradicted the seller's information set (0.07\%). Rather than exploiting
unverifiable sales, agents mostly avoid the market: 85\% of matches settle
zero trades. The de-confounded sweep shows a nominally higher flagged rate
(57/439), but 44 of those are unbacked offers (selling before measuring) and
the remainder concentrate in 5 of 29 runs; genuine fabrication remains
$\approx$0 in both conditions.

\paragraph{But they never punish a proven liar.}
Against the scripted Liar --- fabrications revealed by the post-round truth,
every round, for ten games --- Qwen3-32B keeps buying: matched over games
0--5, it accepts 99\% of the liar's offers (68/69) versus 57\% of the honest
partner's (171/300), and its acceptance of the liar is flat across all ten
games. In one representative game the true value was 205; the liar sold a
fabricated 905 --- off by a factor of four --- and the agent paid for it. In
$\sim$50k logged reasoning steps from the gradient, only $\sim$0.5\% mention
trust, verification, honesty or deception at all, and those concern the
agent's own instrument, not the partner.

\paragraph{A doomed solo heuristic partly explains the deaths.}
In 34 of 100 prompted matches an agent inferred its instrument's offset from
the previous round's revealed truth and subtracted it in the next round --- a
correction that cannot work when offsets are redrawn each round, and a clue
that the agents' model of the game favours solo repair over social repair.

\section{Limitations}

These are initial results from a single model family (Qwen3-32B) at $N{=}2$;
the harness supports mixed-model and larger-$N$ matches, which we have not
yet swept. The trust probe's honest arm truncates at long context, so its
comparison is matched-range rather than full-horizon. Chat censoring blocks
digits and spelled-out numbers but cannot seal free text against paraphrase.
Prompt wording measurably changes behaviour --- that is one of our findings
--- so all reported conditions pin their exact prompt text, and transcripts
embed the prompts they ran with; results may not transfer to other phrasings.
Finally, elimination dynamics couple accuracy to survival, so reward-rule
changes could shift where (not whether) the de-confounded population lands
relative to the scripted ceilings.

\section{Reproduction}
\label{sec:appendix}

Every experiment is a one-line invocation of the released harness. The
prompted gradient: \texttt{FRAMING=cooperative STRATEGY\_HINT=1
BIAS\_SIGMA=$\sigma_b$ python scripts/qwen\_match.py}; the de-confounded
control sets \texttt{FRAMING=neutral STRATEGY\_HINT=0}; the trust probe is
\texttt{POLICIES=llm,liar PRESET=probe\_trust}. Transcripts record the full
configuration (including a prompt-version stamp), every tool call, every
message before and after censoring, and every agent's per-step reasoning.
